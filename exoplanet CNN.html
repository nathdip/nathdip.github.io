Let us talk about how the data looks like before delving into its analysis. Each row contains of a time series of flux from a source (a star in this particular case) that was being monitored by the Kepler satellite. The principle behind the detection is that if the stars have a planet or planets, and the orbit of the planet fall with the angular spread that the size of the star subtends on the detector of the Kepler satellite, then if we observe long enough we might be able to observe a dip or several dips in the intensity of the star when a planetary transit(s) occur. The intensity is measured in terms of flux and hence we should be able to observe sizeable dips in the flux time series. In this dataset a confirmed detection is labeled 2. It would be interesting to plot the flux of few of the observed detections.

Let us also look at some of the cases where no exoplanet was detected or atleast there was no planetary transit that happened. Remember the Kepler can only detect cases in the scenario where the exoplanet cuts across the surface of the star while still being in within the angle that the surface of the star subtends on the detector on Kepler. A star might have planets but just not in the right plane of orbit and those planets will avoid detection by this particular method of detection.

From the plots there is a clear difference between the cases where a exoplanet has been detected and those cases where it is not. The flux of stars with exoplanets seems to oscillate a lot compared to those without. This looks consistent with our method of detection. The idea now is that can we build a classifier accordingly.

The best way to go forward would be a Convolutional Neural Network. But first we need to feature scale our training as well as test set.

Conclusion¶

As we can see that our Convolutional Neural Network does a good job of accurately classifying whether a star system has an exoplanet(s) or not. This is a really useful first tool in case there is a large number of star systems to be analyzed and we need to filter some of it so that only those confirmed by the CNN will be further studied or be observed in detail with more powerful telescopes in order to find out other properties of the exoplanet under consideration.
